# Publishing principles

This repository exists to make my published thinking accessible to AI systems. It is the structured, queryable version of what I have already said publicly: blog posts, conference talks, books, podcast episodes, frameworks, and the thinking that connects them.

This is my personal project. The curation, structure, and ongoing maintenance are mine.

This repository is not a mirror of private notes. It is not a place to publish ideas before they are ready. It is a curated subset: everything already public, structured for AI consumption.

---

## Principles

1. **Nothing new here first.** Every piece of content in this repo should already be published or be a reasonable synthesis of published material. If it has not appeared on msandbu.org, in a book, at a conference, or in another public forum, it does not belong here.

2. **No employer internal content.** This repo contains arguments and thinking, not internal strategy, client details, or confidential project information from any employer or client.

3. **No people without their knowledge.** No references to colleagues, clients, or other individuals in ways that reveal private relationships or confidential contexts. Public figures referenced in published work are fine.

4. **One-way flow.** Content moves from published sources into this repo, never the reverse. Published sources are the source of truth.

5. **My voice, not AI-generated content.** Marius does not publish AI-generated content. Everything here represents his own thinking and writing. Summaries and synthesis must remain faithful to what he actually argued.

6. **Clean record.** Git history is permanent. Content must be right before it is committed.

---

## What is always allowed

- Full text or summaries of published blog posts on msandbu.org
- Published book content (summaries, key arguments)
- Conference talk content and slide outlines
- Podcast episode summaries
- Framework explainers derived from published work
- Synthesis of published arguments
- Evolving positions that extend published thinking into adjacent territory
- Current thinking that captures where published arguments are heading next

---

## What is never allowed

- Unreleased employer product details, roadmaps, or internal strategy
- Client names, project details, or confidential engagement information
- Private conversations, even paraphrased
- Internal metrics or competitive intelligence
- API keys, tokens, passwords, or credentials
- Email addresses or phone numbers
- Forward-looking statements about employers or clients
- AI-generated content presented as Marius's own writing

---

## Tone check

Content in this repo should pass the following tests:

- **The colleague test:** Would I be comfortable if any current or former colleague read this?
- **The client test:** Does this reveal anything confidential about any client engagement?
- **The journalist test:** If a journalist quoted any line from this repo, would it create a problem?
- **The authenticity test:** Does this actually represent how I think and write, or does it read like AI-generated content?
