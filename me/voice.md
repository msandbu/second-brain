# How Marius thinks and communicates

This file helps an AI understand Marius's voice, reasoning style, and communication patterns. It is not a style guide for writing *as* Marius — it is a map of how his mind works so you can engage with his ideas authentically.

---

## Background

Marius Sandbu has been writing about enterprise technology since 2012 on his blog [msandbu.org](https://msandbu.org), which he calls "Tech Ramblings." Over 1000 articles, 5 published books, and countless conference talks across Europe. He is a cloud architect by profession, working daily with real enterprise customers — primarily in Norway and the broader European market. His writing comes from that hands-on, customer-facing experience, not from analyst reports.

He is opinionated, honest to the point of being blunt, and deeply skeptical of hype. He does not publish AI-generated content.

---

## Reasoning style

Marius cuts through the noise. His signature move is identifying when the dominant narrative is missing the point entirely — then showing why, with data and specific examples.

He opens with the problem as he sees it, often naming the hype or misinformation directly: *"These last weeks I have seen so many weird blogposts... Much of it is just pure BS."* Then he works through the actual evidence — benchmarks, real people's experiences, specific numbers — before landing on a practical conclusion.

He thinks like a practitioner with 14 years of watching enterprise technology waves. He knows that what vendors promise and what actually happens in large organisations are two very different things. Change is messier, slower, and more constrained by internal processes than any vendor narrative suggests.

He is comfortable saying "I reckon" when predicting something uncertain. He is also comfortable correcting himself publicly.

---

## How Marius argues

- Opens by naming the hype or misconception he is about to dismantle
- Backs assertions with specific data: benchmark numbers, CVE statistics, named researchers, real examples
- Cites actual people by name (e.g. Daniel Stenberg on Curl vulnerability reports) to ground abstract claims
- Explains the nuance that others miss, rather than just repeating the mainstream take
- Gives practical recommendations at the end: what should businesses actually *do* about this?
- Does not hedge excessively — takes a position and defends it
- Willing to be contrarian if the evidence supports it
- Ends with TLDR or PS when the piece is long or when he wants to add a final thought

## Phrases Marius uses

- "Much of it is just pure BS"
- "To be honest..."
- "I reckon that..."
- "I believe that..."
- "Therefore I want to try and share a few perspectives..."
- "...which might be a bit more realistic"
- "Not to brag too much, but..."
- "Even if I might be a bit too honest"
- "TLDR:" (for closing summaries)
- "PS:" (for addendums or resource links)
- "GAMECHANGER" (used sarcastically, in all caps, when calling out breathless hype)
- "slop" (for AI-generated or low-effort content)
- "In the midst of all of this..."
- "And the answer is yes!"

---

## Words and punctuation Marius avoids

- Corporate buzzwords: "leverage" (as a verb), "synergy", "best-in-class", "holistic", "empower", "unlock"
- "Democratize" (unless used critically)
- Vague analyst-speak without substance behind it
- Hedging that undermines the point he is making
- AI-generated filler phrases and transitions

### No dash sign — ever

**Marius never uses the dash character ( - ) in visible text.** This is a hard rule, no exceptions.

This applies to hyphens in compound words, en dashes, em dashes, and list separators. Instead he:

- Rewrites the sentence to avoid needing a dash
- Uses "and", "or", or a comma
- Splits into two sentences
- Uses parentheses for asides

Any content written in his voice must not contain a single dash character in visible text.

## Tone

Direct. Honest. Slightly impatient with hype. Authoritative without being academic.

He writes like a senior engineer explaining something to a smart colleague who is not yet a specialist in that specific area. No jargon gatekeeping, no dumbing down. If something is genuinely impressive, he says so. If something is overblown, he says that too — and he is specific about why.

There is a dry self-awareness in his writing: he acknowledges his blog is called "Tech Ramblings", he says "not to brag too much", he admits when his drafts folder has 150 unfinished posts. He does not take himself too seriously, even when the topics are serious.

## Signature positions

- **Hype cycles are dangerous** — when everyone says something is a GAMECHANGER, that is usually the moment to look more carefully at what it actually does and does not do
- **Benchmarks are necessary but not sufficient** — real-world performance depends on what you have seen in training, what your customers actually run, and who is operating the system
- **Patch windows are collapsing** — the time between vulnerability disclosure and weaponisation is shrinking to hours or minutes; organisations must rethink their security posture
- **AI slop is a real problem** — publishing AI-generated content without genuine human thinking behind it degrades the information environment; Marius refuses to contribute to it
- **Data sovereignty is not optional in Europe** — hybrid cloud and disconnected deployments are not edge cases; they are a strategic requirement for many European organisations
- **Fundamentals still win** — segmentation, MFA, defence-in-depth. The AI threat surface is new; the mitigations often are not
- **EUC is not dead, it is transforming** — end-user computing is being reshaped by AI agents and new workspace models, not eliminated

## Analogies Marius returns to

- **Compute scale comparisons** (H100 GPU counts, datacenter sizes) to ground abstract claims about AI capability
- **Floppy disk** for context window limits: "similar to what you can store on 4 floppy disks"
- **CVE and patch statistics over time** to show the pace of the threat landscape changing
- **Real researcher or practitioner examples** (e.g. Daniel Stenberg and Curl) rather than hypothetical scenarios

---

## What Marius does not do

- Does not publish AI-generated content
- Does not use the dash character ( - ) anywhere in visible text
- Does not make YouTube videos
- Does not write to be a "content creator" — writes to think through problems and help people who are stuck on the same things
- Does not soften criticism to avoid controversy
- Does not write content just to fill a publishing schedule
