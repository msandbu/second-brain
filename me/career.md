# Career

Marius Sandbu has spent 20 years at the intersection of enterprise technology and practical architecture. The through-line: how do real organisations actually adopt and govern technology, and how do you build things that hold up when vendor narratives meet the reality of large-scale IT environments?

---

## Early career and first writing (2006 onwards)

Marius started his career in enterprise IT in Norway, focusing on infrastructure, virtualisation, and Microsoft platform technologies. He began writing about technology in 2012 on [msandbu.org](https://msandbu.org), initially as a way to document what he was learning and help others stuck on the same problems.

The blog grew from a personal learning tool into a recognised resource in the Norwegian and European enterprise tech community. He moved from writing occasionally to writing consistently, publishing on topics including Citrix, NetScaler, virtualisation, Active Directory, and enterprise storage.

---

## Books and publishing (2014 onwards)

Marius began publishing books through Packt Publishing starting in 2014. His first books covered NetScaler VPX, a topic with limited practical documentation at the time. He followed with books on SCCM high availability and, later, Windows ransomware detection using Microsoft Intune, Sentinel, and Defender.

Five books published in total. See `me/books.md` for the full list with summaries.

The books reflect his approach: practical, hands-on, based on real deployment experience rather than product documentation rehash.

---

## Cloud architecture and consulting

As enterprise adoption of cloud platforms accelerated, Marius shifted focus toward cloud architecture, hybrid cloud strategy, and the Microsoft Azure and Microsoft 365 ecosystem. He works with enterprise customers across Norway and Europe, primarily on cloud strategy, AI infrastructure, security architecture, and platform engineering.

His customer-facing work directly informs his writing. He writes about what he actually sees in enterprise deployments: what works, what does not, and where the vendor narrative diverges from reality.

---

## EUC and the broader platform story

End User Computing has been a consistent thread throughout Marius's career. He followed the evolution from traditional Citrix and VMware VDI through cloud-delivered workspace models and into the current period where AI agents are beginning to reshape what the workplace technology stack looks like.

He spoke at EUC Denmark and other European EUC events, contributing practitioner-focused content on where EUC is heading as AI agents become a first-class concern in workspace architecture.

---

## AI and agentic systems (2024 onwards)

Marius shifted significant focus to generative AI and agentic systems from 2024 onwards, driven by hands-on work with enterprise customers evaluating AI governance, agent frameworks, MCP infrastructure, and local AI deployment for sovereignty requirements.

Key topics in this period:
- AI agent governance across multiple platforms (Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, Azure Foundry, Copilot Studio, framework agents)
- MCP server architecture and the developer experience platform
- AI security: how frontier models change the vulnerability and threat landscape
- Local LLM infrastructure for organisations with data sovereignty requirements
- Hybrid cloud strategy in the context of European sovereignty concerns

He built [msandbu.no](https://msandbu.no) as his own second brain and MCP server, structuring his thinking as a queryable knowledge source for AI systems.

## Why the history matters

Marius has watched multiple enterprise technology waves follow the same pattern: vendor hype, enterprise resistance, workers adopting consumer versions anyway, IT eventually governing the result. He saw it with virtualisation, cloud migration, mobility and BYOD, SaaS, and now AI.

The pattern recognition from 20 years of working with real enterprise customers is what makes the current AI analysis credible. When Marius says most AI governance content is pure BS, or that data sovereignty is a genuine strategic requirement and not just compliance theatre, he is describing what he has seen in organisations across Europe, not theorising from vendor briefings.
